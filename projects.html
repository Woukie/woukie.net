What happened to WoukieBox and WoukieBox 2?

The first one was a fun experiment that took 10 mins to make. It went down because I was too lazy to restart the backend whenever we had a power outage

The second took 2-3 weeks of my life to develop, and it wasn't just a global chatroom, it had accounts, profile pictures / bios, group chats, DMs, themes, notifications with sound effects, anonymous accounts, a desktop app with an installer, discord-like messages and even had VOIP in the works.

Teleblock

I give a lot of shit to my Minecraft projects. Teleblock was originally in misc because it wasn't THAT special. But with that said, it's probably my most impressive Minecraft projects to date. And since I wrote so much about it, I thought it deserved its own section.

Teleblock was a genuinely fun minigame, it was also my last server project before I shut everything down and focused on modding.

The whole gimmick was that blocks teleported when you broke them, hence the name. Do you get why it's called Teleblock? It's called TELE-block because the BLOCKS get TELE-ported. That's why it's called TELE-BLOCK.

The premise was simple, players break blocks to get points, kill players to steal points and use points to get power ups and upgrade their tools. A pretty simple loop that lead to some pretty fun gameplay, no player could get much of an edge and anyone could turn the game around at any moment so it was incredibly intense. Kind of like Mario Cart in that sense, especailly with all the powerups I added.

It also took place on a huge vertical collumn of a floating island, which took up half the development time to make. It had mines, a volcano, a mushroom layer, a deep dark, hidden rooms, a hot spring, camp sites and lots of attention to detail.

As you played, events caused the world to get destroyed around you, with the severity and frequency of events increasing as time went on. Games got really chaotic, and it was fun to see the world get completley destroyed around you.

This project took weeks to complete and test. It was integrated into a larger server with other games of similar effort. I' a'm trying to find the project files and If I find them I'll put it on GitHub.

When I think of this project, I'm incredibly nostalgic about it, It's a shame I can't find the map anywhere.
